Kim Hak-sun

This page is dedicated to Kim Hak-sun, who is renowned for her courageous role in bringing to light the suffering of "comfort women" and her relentless pursuit of justice for the survivors of Japanese military sexual slavery during World War II.

About

Kim Hak-sun was born in 1924 in Korea, which was under Japanese colonial rule at the time. She was a former Korean comfort woman and a prominent activist advocating for acknowledgment and reparations.

Facts

Kim Hak-sun was forcibly taken to a Japanese military comfort station during World War II, where she endured severe sexual abuse and exploitation. In 1991, she became the first former comfort woman to publicly testify about her experiences, a pivotal moment that helped shed light on the horrific abuses faced by over 200,000 women. Her brave testimony was instrumental in raising global awareness about the plight of comfort women and ignited a movement demanding justice and compensation from the Japanese government. Kim Hak-sun's advocacy has been crucial in the ongoing fight for historical redress and support for survivors, ensuring that their suffering is recognized and remembered.
