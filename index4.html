Song Sin-do

This page is dedicated to Song Sin-do, a courageous survivor and activist who has dedicated her life to seeking justice and recognition for the victims of wartime sexual slavery.

About

Song Sin-do was born in 1924 in Korea, which was under Japanese colonial rule at the time. She was a former Korean comfort woman who became an influential advocate for the rights of survivors of sexual slavery by the Japanese military during World War II.

Facts

Song Sin-do was forcibly taken to a Japanese military comfort station during the war, where she suffered severe exploitation and abuse. For many years, she endured the trauma of her experiences in silence. In the 1990s, Song Sin-do began to speak out publicly, sharing her story and advocating for justice for herself and other survivors. Her testimony and activism were instrumental in bringing international attention to the issue of comfort women and in the movement for reparations and a formal apology from the Japanese government.
