Kim Bok-dong

This page is dedicated to Kim Bok-dong, a courageous activist who devoted her life to advocating for justice and acknowledgment for the survivors of Japanese military sexual slavery during World War II.

About

Kim Bok-dong was born in 1926 in Korea, which was then under Japanese rule. She was a former Korean comfort woman and became a prominent voice in the struggle for recognition and reparations for former comfort women.

Facts

Kim Bok-dong was forcibly recruited into a Japanese military comfort station when she was a teenager, where she suffered severe sexual abuse and exploitation. After the war, she faced years of silence and stigma, but in the 1990s, Kim began to publicly share her experiences. Her brave decision to speak out was pivotal in the global movement for justice for comfort women. Through her activism, Kim Bok-dong highlighted the urgent need for a formal apology and compensation from the Japanese government. Her relentless efforts have been instrumental in raising awareness and advocating for the rights of survivors, ensuring their stories are heard and their suffering acknowledged.
